IDSE103 - Assignment #1
Mary Hannah Duhon and Scott's Game

Make Them Fly!


Players: 4-16 players; groups of 2

Materials:

Paper

Markers

Tape

Instructions:

Step one – time limit 2 minutes

Your mission is to work together with your team mate in a private area

Build an object from paper that will fly as far as possible

Do not let anyone see your object—remember, this is top secret

You may use as much paper and tape as you like

Your object must be decorate in some way so that it distinguishes your object

Do not test (fly or toss) your object until step two

Get to work and then meet outside for a contest with the group in 2 minutes


Step two – time limit 4 minutes

Everyone meets outside for flight

Each group makes their object take flight one after the other

Determine which team’s object went the farthest, and which the least

For the team that went farthest, what worked well for you?

For the team that went the least far, what didn’t work well for you?


Step three – time limit 6 minutes

Your mission is to work together in a public and large group area

Build an object from paper that will fly as far as possible

You may prototype, test, and iterate your idea as many times as you like

You may use as much paper and tape as you like

Your object must be decorate in some way so that it distinguishes your object

Look at other team’s prototypes. Steal ideas.

Show others your prototypes and ask for input.

Make your object fly during a test within the time limit.

Iterate your prototype based on your own tests and feedback from others as many times as possible within the time limit.


Step four – time limit 4 minutes

Everyone meets outside for flight

Each group makes their object take flight one after the other

Determine which team’s object went the farthest, and which the least


Group discussion

How did prototyping and testing help?

How did it feel working in secret vs. working openly?

How does this apply to our work at AC4D?
